Maori Culture in Aotearoa New Zealand

Te Reo Maori

Te Reo Maori is one of the official languages of New Zealand. It is an important part of Maori identity, culture, and history.

Many people learn Te Reo Moari to show respect for Maori culture and to help keep the language strong for future generations.

Matariki

Matariki marks the Maori New Year. It is a special time for remembering the past celebrating the present, and planning for the future.

During Matariki, families and communities may come together to share food, tell stories, remembered loved ones, and think about their hopes for the year ahead.

Kapa Haka

Kapa Haka is a traditional Maori performing art. It includes singing, chanting, movement, facial expressions, and group performance.

Kapa Haka helps people express identity, pride, teamwork, and connection to Maori culture.
